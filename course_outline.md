# GLD2O — Discovering the Workplace (Asynchronous Online)

This package contains:
- Course outline with pacing and release cadence
- Pika-ready assignments (A1–A10) organized into 5 units
- Universal rubric (/15) used for all submissions
- Resources folder (templates, guides)

## Pacing & Weekly Rhythm
- Course runs ~17 instructional weeks (adjust for breaks and holidays)
- Daily: students complete a short Pika check-in while progressing on the current assignment.
- Weekly: post a short video (2–4 minutes) previewing the plan, deadlines, and any clarifications.

## Units & Assignment Sequence

### Unit 1: Getting Started
- Week 1: A1 Digital Scavenger Hunt (5%)
- Week 2: A2 Personal Interest Proposal (10%)

### Unit 2: Skills for Success
- Week 3: A3 Essential Skills (5%)
- Weeks 4–5: A4 Self-Management & Teamwork (10%)

### Unit 3: Knowing Yourself & Exploring Careers
- Week 6: A5 Self-Assessment (5%)
- Weeks 7–8: A6 Career Exploration (10%)

### Unit 4: Planning & Preparing
- Weeks 9–10: A7 Decision-Making & Barriers (10%)
- Week 11: A8 Career Plan & Workplace Awareness (5%)
- Weeks 12–13: A9 Job Search Documents (10%)

### Unit 5: Showcase
- Weeks 14–17: A10 Final Portfolio — Google Site (30%)

Final portfolio due end of Week 17.

## Assessment Weights (suggested)
- A1 Digital Scavenger Hunt: 5%
- A2 Personal Interest Proposal: 10%
- A3 Essential Skills: 5%
- A4 Self-Management & Teamwork: 10%
- A5 Self-Assessment: 5%
- A6 Career Exploration: 10%
- A7 Decision-Making & Barriers: 10%
- A8 Career Plan & Workplace Awareness: 5%
- A9 Job Search Documents: 10%
- A10 Final Portfolio (Google Site): 30%
- **Total: 100%**
- All use the universal /15 rubric (Completion, Thinking/Communication, Workflow/Authenticity).

## Submission Expectations
- All assignments (except A10) are completed in Pika using the assignments feature.
- A10 Final Portfolio is a Google Site; students submit the URL in Pika.
- At the end of the course, final portfolio links will be shared with classmates.
- Pika tracks history automatically; encourage students to show their process.
