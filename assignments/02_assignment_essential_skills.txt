Assignment 2 — Essential Skills Application Task

Purpose: practice the workplace essential skills and show how you apply them to real tasks.

Learning goals:
- Identify key workplace essential skills and where they show up in jobs you care about.
- Apply literacy/numeracy and communication strategies to complete tasks.
- Reflect on how these skills transfer between school, community, and work.

What to do (complete in the assigned Google Doc):
1) Skill snapshot: choose 3 essential skills (e.g., reading text, document use, numeracy, writing, oral communication, thinking skills, working with others, computer use). For each, note where you’ve used it and where it’s required in a job you’re interested in.
2) Apply the skills: complete BOTH mini-tasks below and paste your work in the Doc.
   - Scheduling: create a 5-day schedule for balancing school, this course, and one personal responsibility. Include start/end times and priorities.
   - Budgeting/estimation: plan a $150 budget for a small project (e.g., club event, hobby kit). Show simple calculations and trade-offs.
3) Reflection (8–10 sentences): what was easy/hard, how the skills transfer, and one next step to improve.

Deliverable:
- The assigned Google Doc with all three sections completed.

How to submit:
- Attach/turn in your Doc in Google Classroom.
- Daily: log what you worked on in Pika (1–2 sentences).

Success criteria (universal /15 rubric):
- Completion: snapshot, both mini-tasks, and reflection are present.
- Thinking/communication: calculations are shown; explanations are clear and specific.
- Workflow/authenticity: organized Doc with headings, visible work (tables/lists), daily Pika check-ins.
