GLD2O — Conduct

Asynchronous Online Course • Grade 10 • Course Outline

Respect

Be respectful of the learning environment — your classmates, your teacher, and your own learning. In an online course, this means communicating thoughtfully and treating shared spaces with care.

Staying on Schedule

This is an asynchronous course, which means you can work at your own pace within each week's timeframe. However, staying on schedule is essential. Each assignment has a suggested week on the course outline — use that as your guide.

If you fall behind, reach out early. It's much easier to help you get back on track before things pile up.

Communication

Check Pika for the calendar of work and due dates. This is your main reference for what's coming up and when things are due.

If you have questions or need to reach me, email stewart.chan@yrdsb.ca. For assignment-specific questions, include the assignment number in your subject line.

Attendance

Attendance in this course is taken through Pika. Each class day, you must complete the "Today" section in Pika, which logs your daily activity. Once that section is filled in, you are marked present. If it is not attempted, you are marked absent.

You have until the end of the day (before midnight) to complete your daily attendance. Make it part of your routine — log in, complete "Today", then continue working on your assignments.

Tech Requirements

You need a computer with internet access.

Working in Pika

All assignments (A1–A9) are completed inside Pika, the learning platform for this course. A10 is a Google Site. Pika tracks your work history, including when and how you build your responses over time.

Your workflow matters. Assignments should show progressive work — evidence that you developed your responses over time, not all at once in a single session. You don't need to be perfect on the first try. Drafting, revising, and improving your work is expected and encouraged.

All of your work should be original. You are welcome to use resources — including classmates, websites, and AI tools — to help you learn and develop your ideas. However, you are not allowed to copy and paste someone else's work and submit it as your own. A submission that appears as a single large paste with no prior drafting will be flagged for review. This isn't about catching you — it's about ensuring fairness for everyone.

Do not share your login or password with anyone. Any activity under your account is your responsibility.
